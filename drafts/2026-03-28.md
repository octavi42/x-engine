## 5. Thread hook + outline (Claude driving Perseverance on Mars)

**Hook:**

NASA used Claude to plan Perseverance rover drives on Mars. the AI analyzed orbital images, wrote waypoints in Rover Markup Language, and guided the rover 1,496 feet across Martian terrain. a task humans did manually for 28 years.

<!-- 231 chars -->

**Thread outline:**

1. Hook (above)
2. How it works: JPL engineers fed years of driving data and terrain images to Claude Code. Claude used vision to analyze overhead shots and wrote drive commands in RML — the rover's bespoke XML-based language.
3. The drives: Dec 8 (689 ft) and Dec 10 (807 ft) on sols 1707 and 1709. Claude planned ten-meter segments, iterated on its own waypoints, critiqued and revised before submission.
4. Verification: every AI-generated command ran through JPL's digital twin — a virtual replica of the rover that checks 500,000+ telemetry variables before anything gets sent to Mars.
5. Result: engineers only needed minor changes. estimated 50% reduction in route-planning time going forward.
6. Why it matters: Mars has a ~20 min comms delay. autonomous planning unlocks missions to Europa and Titan where the lag is hours, not minutes.
7. Tie to building: same pattern as Clio — AI analyzing spatial context, generating output autonomously, humans reviewing before execution. the abstraction scales from walking around Bucharest to driving on Mars.
