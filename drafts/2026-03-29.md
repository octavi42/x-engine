## 5. Thread hook + outline (OpenAI killed Sora)

**Hook:**

OpenAI shut down Sora permanently. the most hyped AI demo of 2024 couldn't survive its own inference costs. all that compute is now going to robotics research. a product that generated hype but not sustainable unit economics.

<!-- 225 chars -->

**Thread outline:**

1. Hook (above)
2. Why it died: video generation is orders of magnitude more compute-intensive than text or images. the cost per generated minute made it unprofitable at any consumer price point.
3. The pivot: OpenAI is redirecting GPU capacity toward robotics — physical-world AI that can generate revenue through hardware partnerships and enterprise contracts, not just $20/mo subscriptions.
4. Pattern recognition: same arc as Google Stadia. impressive tech demo, real engineering achievement, unsustainable unit economics, shutdown.
5. Who survives in AI video: Runway, Pika, and Kling focused on short production clips and enterprise workflows instead of open-ended consumer generation. smaller scope = manageable costs.
6. The lesson for builders: before shipping an AI feature, model the inference cost per user per month. if the math doesn't work at 10x your current user base, it won't work at scale.
7. Tie to building: this is why Clio uses ElevenLabs for voice instead of building a custom model. outsource the expensive inference, own the product layer on top.
